# Upma
A simple Gujrati (Indian) food usually served for breakfast, but always tasty. This version uses the instant pot. It is my own variation, adding extra nuts for protein and sweet potato over a more traditional potato. If you want even more protein, garnish with hemp hearts.

## Ingredients
- 1 c farina (dry toasted)
- 2 tbs ghee
- 1 medium sweet potato (diced)
- 2 tsp black mustard seeds
- 2 tsp cumin seeds
- 1 tbsp grated ginger
- 2 serrano peppers, seeded and diced (or 1/2 tsp cayenne)
- 1 tsp tumeric
- 1 1/2 tsp salt
- 1/2 c cashew pieces
- 3 c boiling hot water
- 2 tbs lemon juice (half a lemon)
- 1 bunch cilantro, destemmed and chopped
- 1 c frozen peas (defrosted)
- hemp hearts (optional garnish, 1-3 tbsp per serving)

## Directions
1. Dry toast the farina in a pan and transfer to a bowl so it won't burn. 
2. Set instant pot to sautee.
3. Add ghee, let it get hot.
4. Add mustard seeds and cumin, toast until mustard seeds begin to pop
5. Stir in ginger, tumeric, pepper and salt, sautee for 2 more minutes. Stirring to keep spices from sticking.
6. Stir in diced sweet potato. Add some water to pan to steam cook the potatoes until they are soft.
7. Turn off sautee.
8. Add boiling water. Add cashews. Stir in farina slowly so it doesn't clump.
9. Close lid, setting instant pot to pressure cook for 3 minutes.
10. After cooking is done, add in lemon juice, peas and cilantro.
11. Let sit for 5 minutes with lid closed before serving.
